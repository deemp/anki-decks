About this deck

This deck is based on:

  • A Frequency Dictionary of German: Core Vocabulary for Learners (Routledge Frequency Dictionaries), 2nd Edition, 2019, by Erwin Tschirner and Jupp Möhring (link)
  • The deck English-Deutsch (Sorted by Frequency)

This deck contains approximately 5,000 German words that occurred most frequently in the corpus studied by the authors. The most frequent words appear at the beginning of the deck.

The cards include word forms, AI-generated German sentences, AI-generated English translations, and AI-generated audio.

The text-to-speech version of this deck is available here.

Fields

  • index - The card number in the deck. These numbers start from 0. Cards are sorted by this field.
  • frequency_rank - Frequency rank (as per the book's "Rank frequency"). The more frequent a word is in the studied corpus, the higher the rank and the lower the frequency_rank number. Cards with the same frequency_rank have consecutively increasing index values.
  • part_of_speech_short - One of the following:
    • A part of speech or its abbreviation.
    • Possible articles of a noun.
  • word_de - A German word.
  • word_forms_de - Forms of the German word:
    • For a noun - its plural form.
    • For a verb - its principal parts.
    • For a pronoun (if applicable) - its declensions.
  • sentence_de - A short (30 to 40 characters long) German sentence that includes the word.
  • word_translations_en - English translations of the German word.
  • word_en - One of the translations from word_translations_en. This field may be empty if word_translations_en is empty.
  • sentence_en - An English translation of the German sentence. This translation includes word_en.
  • part_of_speech - Part of speech.
  • sentence_original_de - The original German sentence from the book.
  • sentence_original_en - The original English sentence from the book.
  • word_sound_de - Audio of word_de.
  • sentence_sound_de - Audio of sentence_de.
  • word_sound_en - Audio of word_en.
  • sentence_sound_en - Audio of sentence_en.

AI-generated sentences

The sentences in the sentence_de and sentence_en fields were generated using ChatGPT 4.0 mostly in August - October 2024. I selected sentences that sounded natural and seemed useful for learning.

I decided to generate sentences because I wanted full control over their content and length. I needed control over the content because I felt the original sentences provided more context than necessary to understand a word. I also needed control over the sentence length to ensure sentences could be learned in a reasonable amount of time.

Since I am not a native German or English speaker, some sentences may contain errors.

AI-generated audio

The audio was generated using the HyperTTS plugin for the desktop version of Anki. I used free Google Translate voices.

Update the deck

You can update the deck without downloading a new version from AnkiWeb.

  1. Copy the data from this file into a local file.
  2. Import the local file into the deck using the appropriate note type. In the Import options, set:
    • Existing notes: Update
    • Match scope: Notetype and deck
  3. Update the audio using the HyperTTS plugin for the desktop version of Anki (see 1, 2, 3). Tips:
    • Use free Google Translate voices to avoid regenerating the audio.
    • Create presets to simplify future updates.
    • Select all cards and update the audio. HyperTTS will generate audio only for fields that have changed.

Do not add new words to this deck

Since you don't know the frequency ranks of new words, you won’t be able to insert them into the correct positions.

Instead, create a new deck where you can insert or append new cards.
